Introduction / Brief

The directors Leicestershire based at Media Design agency ‘Seed Creativity’ ran a module on my university degree called “Creative Media Entrepreneurship”. As part of the module we were required to choose an existing employer and pitch a product idea or campaign. Having undertaken research on some potential employers for the assignment I had come across a vast number of digital design agencies in Leicestershire. I noticed Seed Creativity themselves had fallen short of a strong digital presence that their competitors possessed. Their current site didn’t provide their consumers with usable and rich experience which is required today. The need for a re-design was evident.

Seed Creativity required a responsive and bespoke re-design to connect more effectively with their consumer base. I pitched this idea to company directors,which they loved. The plans for development of the re-design shortly commenced.

Design Process

User Research

The project undertaken in a very agile and iterative approach as both company directors and I consistently arranged regular meetings to maintain a high level communication. Tools such as Trello, Google Docs and Invisionapp were key in the management and auditing throughout the design process. The project began by fleshing out business objectives company directors and began which then led to undertaking qualitative research methods such as user surveys and observations to understand the type of users I was required to design for.

Information Architecture

Once we knew who exactly we were designing for it enabled me and the company directors devise a strong information architecture for the new re-design by creating site maps, content audits and wireframes.

Visual Design

Using the information gathered from the previous stages, I able to produce a high level visual pixel-perfect mockups and interaction storyboards for both mobile and desktop views using Photoshop. A lot of the project was spent on this phase due to large number of iterations request by the product owners. These designs were then presented to an external company called Effect Digital for review as they were going to be involved in the back-end development of the site.

Development

I then built a responsive prototype using HTML,CSS, Sass & jQuery. Versions of this prototype was committed regularly through git and allowing the back-end developers to track to the process.
