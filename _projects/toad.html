Toad London is a young and emerging creative agency that produces advertising campaigns, websites, mobile apps and content for brands. Since the turn of the year 2016, Toad wanted to undertake a new strategy to win over new business by using their recent successes on projects as the focal point of their sales pitch. With this in mind, the company directors felt their current site didn't align with their and wanted to undertake a website re-design which includes the following feature set:

  • Case Studies – To sell the narrative and process behind each project.

  • Animation Driven – To provide a engaging experience for users and showcase what they can do.

  • Minimal – In style but effective and memorable.

  • Project Date

    Early 2016

  • Project Duration

    Two months

  • Contributors

    Daniel Montgomery – Visual Design

    Myself & Kevin Rousseau – Front-End Development

Choosing A Suitable Framework

Having analysed the requirements company directors, we (the development team) came to the conclusion that using a framework or library on this project would be a good idea as this will be idea as we were on a tight schedule. We chose a JavaScript framework called Vue.js for these following reasons:

  • Small in file size & fast rendering – Ideal for this project as this is a small site.

  • Easy to learn

  • A focus on the view layer in MVC pattern – This site is purely for visual and marketing purposes so the fact the library has a focus on the presentational layer meshes well with the goal of the site. This also limits the chances of library giving us unnecessary features we don't we need.

  • Preserves many of the core JavaScript MVC features – such as asynchronous page loading (routing), event binding and enforces modular written code by building components.

Design Began On The Computer

High fidelity mock-ups of the site were created very early in the design process. This approach forced many avoidable design revisions that could have been discovered at lower design level (wireframes) or even through more thorough discovery process. This approach also caused a lot of back and forth between design and development as mock-ups were being built as they were being designed due to being on a tight schedule.

Modular Approach To Design

Combating the constant design revisions whilst maintaining on track of the development schedule we executed the following:

  • Dissect the design into smaller pieces – Analysed any repetition, behaviour, relationships between UI elements such as buttons in the design and built them as reusable web components and templates.

  • Write modular CSS code – that was flexible for design revisions and reusable.

  • Descriptive and meaningful folder names – This made navigating around folders easy and fast thus allowing us to be more productive.

  • Challenged design revisions – Some revisions were purely aesthetic that didn't provide any value to the site going forward, those were the ones we challenged.
