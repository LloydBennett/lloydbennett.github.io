Toad London is a young and emerging creative agency that produces advertising campaigns, websites, mobile apps and content for brands. Since the turn of the year 2016, Toad wanted to undertake a new strategy to win over new business by using their recent successes on projects as the focal point of their sales pitch. With this in mind, the company directors felt their current site didn't align with their goals and wanted to undertake a website re-design which includes the following feature set:

  • Case Studies – To sell the narrative and process behind each project.

  • Animation Driven – To provide a engaging experience for users and showcase what they can do.

  • Minimal – In style but effective and memorable.

  • Project Date

    Early 2016

  • Project Duration

    Two months

  • Contributors

    Daniel Montgomery – Visual Design

    Myself & Kevin Rousseau – Front-End Development

Choosing A Suitable Framework

Having analysed the requirements company directors, we (the development team) came to the conclusion that using a framework or library on this project would be a good idea as this will be idea as we were on a tight schedule. We chose a JavaScript framework called Vue.js for these following reasons:

  • Small in file size & fast rendering – Ideal for this project as this is a small site.

  • Easy to learn

  • A focus on the view layer in MVC pattern – This site is purely for visual and marketing purposes so the fact the library has a focus on the presentational layer meshes well with the goal of the site. This also limits the chances of library giving us unnecessary features we don't we need.

  • Preserves many of the core JavaScript MVC features – such as asynchronous page loading (routing), event binding and enforces modular written code by building components.

Design Began On The Computer

High fidelity mock-ups of the site were created very early in the design process. This approach forced many avoidable design revisions that could have been discovered at lower design level (wireframes) or even through more thorough discovery process. This approach also caused a lot of back and forth between design and development as mock-ups were being built as they were being designed due to being on a tight schedule.

Modular Approach To Design

Combating the constant design revisions whilst maintaining on track of the development schedule we executed the following:

  • Dissect the design into smaller pieces – Analysed any repetition, behaviour, relationships between UI elements such as buttons in the design and built them as reusable web components and templates.

  • Write modular CSS code – that was flexible for design revisions and reusable.

  • Descriptive and meaningful folder names – This made navigating around folders easy and fast thus allowing us to be more productive.

  • Challenged design revisions – Some revisions were purely aesthetic that didn't provide any value to the site going forward, those were the ones we challenged.

Outcome

The website was delivered and was able to support the companies' forthcoming sales pitches. The company was able to refer potential clients to our site in conversation with great confidence.

Final Thoughts

Reflecting on this project although was able to deliver a marketing site of high quality that represents the Toad brand well, I felt there was a number of areas where we failed on the project:

  • Was a JavaScript Framework really needed?– The sort answer is no. Looking back at what we built in the end we didn't build anything nearly as sophisticated and large enough to suggest a JavaScript framework. In fact using a framework was actually an overkill as there was a lot features were not essential to the project and we constantly broke out of the frameworks' structure to meet our needs. We were actually better of writing the features we needed ourselves.

  • We didn't validate whether our solution was successful – When building a solution to a problem it's essential that you validate whether the experiment was successful or not. After the project completed there was no evidence of that as we focused sorely on whether the company directors felt the final outcome deemed fit to launch and not test the solution with the targeted audience.

  • Design Started On The Computer – This approach to design created a number of issues in project that caused us to push on back on deadlines. The design process is arguably one of the most crucial aspects to the success or failure project as it paves the direction as to what will get built. This isn't something you want to skip or rush even if you have a time constraint. Undertaking a more thorough design process would have been beneficial to us as it would have removed a lot of the guesswork that resulted in constant design revisions as well as provided us with the opportunity validate our initial ideas on a solution with our targeted audience. Through this process we may have found out that a entire redesign was not necessary for our problem and perhaps minor changes would have suffice. This would have been more cost effective for the company going forward.
